## Getting Started

### What is StoryAI?

StoryAI is an AI-powered storyboard creation platform designed for filmmakers, directors, producers, and creative teams. It automates the process of converting scripts into visual storyboards while maintaining full creative control over every shot.

**Key Benefits:**
- **Fast**: Transform scripts into storyboards in minutes instead of days
- **Smart**: AI automatically parses scripts and detects scenes and shots
- **Flexible**: Full creative control with detailed cinematography customization
- **Professional**: Generate high-quality frames tailored to your vision
- **Collaborative**: Share and manage projects with your team

### System Requirements

- **Browser**: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge (latest versions)
- **Internet**: Stable broadband connection
- **File Format**: Plain text or `.pdf` screenplays
- **Optional**: Graphics tablet for enhanced editing (not required)

### Accessing StoryAI

1. Visit [storyai.com](https://storyai.com)
2. Click **"Get Started"** or **"Sign In"** in the top navigation
3. Choose your preferred authentication method (see Account Management below)

## Uploading Scripts

### Supported Formats

- **Plain Text** (.txt) - Fastest upload
- **PDF** (.pdf) - Industry standard screenplays
- **Standard Formats**:
  - Final Draft (.fountain)
  - Screenwriting software exports
  - Plain text in screenplay format

### How to Upload

1. Open your project (click **"Open"** on the project card)
2. You'll see the **Editor** with an empty script area
3. Click **"Upload Script"** or drag-and-drop a file
4. Select your screenplay file
5. Click **"Parse Script"** (AI will analyze it)

### What Gets Parsed

The AI automatically extracts:
- **Scenes**: Scene headings and locations
- **Shots**: Individual shots or actions within scenes
- **Characters**: Named characters and their dialogue
- **Action**: Physical descriptions and cinematography notes

### Understanding Parse Results

After parsing, you'll see:

```
Scene 1: INT. COFFEE SHOP - DAY
├── Shot 1: Wide establishing shot of coffee shop
├── Shot 2: Close-up of character entering
└── Shot 3: Two-shot of characters at table

Scene 2: EXT. STREET - NIGHT
├── Shot 1: Character leaving building
└── Shot 2: Walking down empty street
```

You can:
- **Edit** shot descriptions
- **Delete** unnecessary shots
- **Add** new shots manually
- **Rearrange** shot order by dragging

## Customizing Shots

### Editing Shot Captions

The **caption** is the main description of the shot, visible in the storyboard.

1. Click on the shot in the left panel
2. In the right panel, edit the **Caption** field
3. Changes auto-save (you'll see a save indicator)

### Setting Cinematography Details

#### Camera Angle
Describes the vertical perspective of the camera:
- **High Angle**: Camera pointing downward (subjects appear small/vulnerable)
- **Eye Level**: Camera at character eye height (neutral perspective)
- **Low Angle**: Camera pointing upward (subjects appear large/powerful)
- **Dutch Angle**: Tilted horizon (creates tension/unease)

#### Shot Size
Defines how much of the subject is visible:
- **Wide/Establishing**: Shows full location and context
- **Medium**: Shows subject and surroundings
- **Close-up**: Shows face or details only
- **Extreme Close-up**: Shows small detail only

#### Lens Type
Affects perspective distortion:
- **Wide-Angle** (24mm-35mm): Expansive view, some distortion
- **Standard** (50mm): Natural human perspective
- **Telephoto** (85mm-135mm): Compressed perspective, flatters faces

#### Time of Day
Affects lighting and color palette:
- **Dawn**: Golden/pink hues, soft shadows
- **Morning**: Bright, cool shadows
- **Afternoon**: Harsh shadows, bright light
- **Sunset**: Orange/purple hues, dramatic light
- **Night**: Dark, artificial lighting

### Editing Production Notes

The **Notes** section lets you add context for your team:
- Specific mood or emotion for the shot
- Actor directions
- Special equipment needed
- Lighting setup notes
- Props or set dressing

**Example Note:**
```
Keep atmosphere tense and claustrophobic.
Use practical lighting from desk lamp only.
Actor should play subtle nervousness.
```

### Using the Prompt Editor

The **AI Prompt** is what generates your image. You can:

1. **View Default Prompt**: Automatically generated from your inputs
2. **Edit Manually**: Click in prompt editor to add/remove details
3. **Use Keywords**: Add specific visual styles or techniques

**Example Prompts:**
```
Wide shot of minimalist apartment, morning sunlight, 
clean lines, calm atmosphere, cinematic lighting.

Close-up of actor's face, expression of shock, 
dramatic side lighting, film noir style, high contrast.

Establishing shot of abandoned warehouse, dusk light, 
cool blue tones, industrial aesthetic, atmospheric fog.
```

**Prompt Tips:**
- Be specific about mood and lighting
- Include style references if desired
- Mention character emotions
- Describe environmental elements

## Managing Takes

### What Are Takes?

A **take** is a different version of the same shot. Multiple takes give you options:

- Generated automatically when you create/regenerate images
- Each take is timestamped and tagged
- You select which take appears in final export
- Useful for A/B testing visual styles

### Viewing Takes

1. Click on a shot
2. In the center panel, below the main image
3. You'll see **Takes Gallery** with all variations
4. Hover over any take to preview

### Selecting Your Favorite Take

1. View the takes gallery
2. Click the **checkmark icon** on your preferred take
3. That take becomes the "selected take" (highlighted)
4. This is the version that exports in your final storyboard

### Regenerating Takes

To create more variations:

1. Adjust the **prompt** or **cinematography settings**
2. Click **"Generate Again"** (or "Regenerate Image")
3. New take is added to the gallery
4. You can now compare multiple versions

### Deleting Takes

To clean up takes:

1. Hover over unwanted take
2. Click **trash icon**
3. Confirm deletion (cannot be undone)
4. At least one take must remain per shot

### Tips for Managing Takes

- **Keep Best Versions**: Save your 2-3 favorite takes per shot
- **Document Decisions**: Use notes to explain why you chose certain takes
- **Save Time**: Delete failed generations to keep file sizes small
- **Comparison**: Keep similar takes together for A/B comparison

## Exporting Your Storyboard

### Export Formats

#### PDF Export
- **Best For**: Printing, presentations, sharing with stakeholders
- **Includes**: All shots in order with captions
- **Layout**: One or multiple shots per page
- **File Size**: Small (good for email)

#### ZIP Export
- **Best For**: Archiving, detailed backups, further editing
- **Includes**: High-res images, captions, metadata
- **File Size**: Larger (all images included)
- **Bonus**: Can re-import into StoryAI later

### How to Export

**From Project Dashboard:**
1. Right-click project card
2. Click **"Download"**
3. Select format (PDF or ZIP)
4. Click **"Export"**
5. File downloads automatically

**From Editor:**
1. Click **"File"** menu (top-left)
2. Click **"Export Project"**
3. Choose format and options
4. Click **"Download"**

### Export Options

**Page Layout (PDF only):**
- 1 shot per page (detailed)
- 2 shots per page (compact)
- 4 shots per page (thumbnail view)

**Include Metadata:**
- ☑ Shot captions
- ☑ Shot details (angle, lens, etc.)
- ☑ Production notes
- ☑ Timestamp information

**Image Quality:**
- Standard (web-friendly, smaller files)
- High (print-quality, larger files)

### Sharing Without Exporting

**Share Project Link (Creator+ Plan):**
1. Click **"Share"** on project card
2. Enable **"Share Link"**
3. Choose permissions:
   - View Only
   - Can Comment
   - Can Edit
4. Copy link and send to team
5. Recipient can view/edit in browser

## Best Practices

### Script Preparation

**Before Uploading:**
- ✅ Use proper screenplay format (industry standard)
- ✅ Include scene headings (INT/EXT, LOCATION, TIME)
- ✅ Number scenes or provide clear breaks
- ✅ Include character names for dialogue scenes
- ❌ Avoid mixing screenplay with personal notes

**Example Format:**
```
INT. COFFEE SHOP - DAY

The shop is busy but cozy. ALEX (30s, thoughtful)
sits at a corner table with a laptop.

JORDAN (early 30s, energetic) enters and spots ALEX.

JORDAN
Hey! Didn't expect to see you here.

ALEX
(looking up)
Jordan! How are you?
```

### Efficient Storyboarding

**Workflow Tips:**
1. **Parse First**: Let AI extract scenes/shots
2. **Quick Review**: Scan all shots before customization
3. **Batch Customize**: Set similar shots together (scene-by-scene)
4. **Generate in Batches**: 5-10 shots at a time
5. **Review & Select**: Pick best takes before moving on
6. **Refine**: Only regenerate shots that need changes

**Time Estimates:**
- Small project (10 shots): 30-45 minutes
- Medium project (25 shots): 2-3 hours
- Large project (50+ shots): 4-6 hours

### Image Generation Best Practices

**Effective Prompts:**
- ✅ Specific mood: "tense," "romantic," "comedic"
- ✅ Lighting details: "soft morning light," "harsh shadows"
- ✅ Environmental details: "busy marketplace," "empty highway"
- ✅ Visual style: "cinematic," "noir," "naturalistic"

**Avoid in Prompts:**
- ❌ Vague descriptions: "good," "nice," "cool"
- ❌ Instruction words: "show," "make," "create"
- ❌ Real person names (generates random faces instead)
- ❌ Copyrighted character descriptions

### Organization Tips

**Project Naming:**
- Include project name and version: "MyFilm_v1_Draft"
- Add date reference: "MyFilm_2024-03-17"
- Keep it searchable: "MyFilm_Final_Approved"

**Using Notes Effectively:**
- Document why you chose specific angles
- Note technical limitations or challenges
- Record feedback from stakeholders
- Mark shots pending approval

### Quality Control

**Before Final Export:**
1. **Review All Shots**: Watch storyboard start to finish
2. **Check Continuity**: Do shots flow logically?
3. **Verify Details**: Do angles match script intentions?
4. **Consistency**: Is visual style consistent?
5. **Completeness**: Are all scenes covered?

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

#### "My script won't upload"

**Possible Causes:**
- File too large (max 10MB)
- Unsupported format
- Corrupted file

**Solutions:**
1. Check file format (TXT or PDF recommended)
2. Try a smaller section of the script
3. Reconvert PDF to text if needed
4. Contact support if persistent

#### "AI parsing missed some shots"

**Possible Causes:**
- Non-standard screenplay format
- Ambiguous action descriptions
- Handwritten or unusual formatting

**Solutions:**
1. Manually add missing shots (click **"+ Add Shot"**)
2. Edit existing shot descriptions
3. Use clearer scene headings
4. Contact support for complex scripts

#### "Generated image doesn't match my vision"

**Solutions:**
1. **Adjust the prompt**: Add more specific details
2. **Change cinematography settings**: Try different angles/lenses
3. **Regenerate**: Create multiple takes and compare
4. **Simplify**: Try shorter, clearer descriptions
5. **Use examples**: Describe similar movies/visual styles

**Example Refinement:**
```
Original: "Scene in a house"
Better: "Cozy living room, warm afternoon sunlight 
through windows, lived-in bohemian style, soft golden 
lighting, intimate atmosphere"
```

#### "Generation is very slow"

**Possible Causes:**
- Server load is high
- Complex or detailed prompts
- Network connection issues

**Solutions:**
1. Wait 15-30 minutes and retry
2. Simplify the prompt
3. Check internet connection
4. Try at different time of day (off-peak hours)
5. Upgrade to Creator+ for priority processing

#### "Changes not saving automatically"

**Possible Causes:**
- Internet disconnected
- Browser cache issues
- Session expired

**Solutions:**
1. Check internet connection
2. Look for **save indicator** (you'll see "Saving..." message)
3. Refresh the page (your changes are backed up)
4. Clear browser cache and reload
5. Use different browser if issue persists

#### "Can't download exported PDF"

**Possible Causes:**
- Browser pop-up blocker
- File too large
- Session timeout

**Solutions:**
1. Check browser pop-up settings (allow for storyai.com)
2. Use ZIP export instead (may be smaller)
3. Wait a few minutes and retry
4. Try different browser

### Performance Optimization

**Browser Tips:**
- Use Chrome or Firefox for best performance
- Keep browser up to date
- Close unnecessary tabs
- Disable browser extensions if having issues

**Network Tips:**
- Use wired connection if possible (more stable than WiFi)
- Avoid VPNs if experiencing slowness
- Check ISP connection quality
